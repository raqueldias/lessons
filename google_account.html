GOOGLE ACCOUNT

you need a google account for this class... here is how to get one

Why do I need a google account?

This course's programming exercises require access to google colab.

In this course, we will be using google colab to run our programming exercises. Google colab is a great platform that allows us to share Python code and access high-performance hardware resources through a web browser. It is free to use but requires a google account to access.

In order to access the programming exercises and design, train and evaluate your AIs, you will need a valid google account.

How do I get a google account for this course?

There are three possible ways to get your google account set up for this course.
  • use your existing personal account
  • create a new google account
  • use your UF credentials to access you UF-associated account

Using an existing account

If you have a @gmail.com email address, then you already have a google account, and you are free to use your existing account for this course.

Creating a new account

If you don't have an existing google account and would like to create a new personal account to use for this course, go to https://accounts.google.com and follow the instructions to create a new account. If google automatically logs you into an account, then you probably already have an account, and you are free to use that one.

Whether you use an existing account or create a new one, this will be your personal google account that you can take with you after you leave UF; you are personally responsible for your account.

Using your UF-associated google account

If you have a University of Florida email address and a gatorlink id, you should have a google account already set up and associated with your UF credentials; you may use your UF-associated google account for this course.

To log into your UF-associated google account, go to https://accounts.google.com.

If you are already logged into a google account and would like to switch to your UF-associated account, click on your account icon in the upper-right corner and either select your UF-associated account from the drop-down list, or click on "Add another account". You will then need to click on "Use another account" and enter your UF email address in the sign-in form. Click the "Next" button, which will take you to the UF login page. Enter your gatorlink id and password, navigate through the two-factor authentication protocol, and you should be logged into your UF-associated google account.

If you are not already logged into a google account, you should see a list of possible accounts. Select your UF-associated account, or click on "Use another account", enter your UF email address, and click the "Next" button. This will take you to the UF login page, where you can enter your gtorlink id and password, navigate two-factor authentication, and then you'll be logged into your UF-associated account.

The instructions on the google accounts page should be the most reliable and up-to-date information about creating and accessing your google accounts.
